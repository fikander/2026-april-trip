The "Alps & Lakes" EV Loop

Overview: Warsaw → South Moravia (CZ) → Julian Alps (SI) → Lake Garda (IT) → Carinthia (AT) → Warsaw
Day 1: Warsaw to South Moravia, Czechia

    Drive: ~550 km (approx. 6–7 hours with a charging stop or two).

    The Route: Head south via Katowice and Ostrava down to the Mikulov/Lednice area near the Austrian border.

    Why here? This is Czechia’s wine region. It’s relatively flat, bathed in spring sunshine, and covered in perfect, paved cycling paths weaving through vineyards and the UNESCO-listed Lednice-Valtice cultural landscape.

    Tesla Tip: There is a Supercharger in Brno, just north of your destination, so you can easily top up before arriving at your hotel.

Day 2: South Moravia to Lake Bled/Bohinj, Slovenia

    Drive: ~450 km (approx. 5 hours).

    The Route: Drive past Vienna and Graz, crossing the border into the majestic Slovenian Alps.

    Activity: Settle into Lake Bled or the quieter, wilder Lake Bohinj. Take an afternoon walk around either lake.

    Tesla Tip: Superchargers are conveniently located near Graz (AT) and Maribor (SI) along the way, plus destination chargers at many Bled hotels.

Day 3: Cycling & Hiking in Triglav National Park (Slovenia)

    No driving today! * Cycling: Cycle the Radovna Valley cycle route. It’s a stunning, mostly flat alpine valley path that avoids major road traffic.

    Hiking: Hike the Vintgar Gorge (usually opens in mid-April) or take the trail up to Ojstrica for the classic, panoramic view of Lake Bled.

Day 4: Slovenia to Lake Garda, Italy

    Drive: ~280 km (approx. 3.5 hours).

    The Route: Drive west through Italy’s Friuli and Veneto regions, heading to Riva del Garda or Torbole at the northern tip of Lake Garda.

    Activity: The northern tip of Garda is a mecca for outdoor sports. Unload the bikes and do a relaxed evening ride along the lakefront paths. Superchargers are located in Affi and Rovereto South.

Day 5: Lake Garda (Italy)

    No driving today!

    Cycling: Ride the Strada del Ponale. It’s an old road carved into the cliffside that has been converted exclusively for cyclists and pedestrians. It offers breathtaking views of the lake.

    Hiking: If you want a hike, take the cable car up from Malcesine to Monte Baldo. Even if there is a little snow at the very top, the lower trails offer spectacular views over the lake.

Day 6: Lake Garda to Carinthia, Austria

    Drive: ~350 km (approx. 4.5 hours).

    The Route: Head north through the Brenner Pass into Austria, cutting east toward Villach or Lake Wörthersee.

    Activity: This is Austria's southernmost province, known for its sunny microclimate and warm lakes.

    Tesla Tip: You can easily charge at the Villach Supercharger.

Day 7: The Austrian Lake District

    No driving today!

    Cycling: Ride a section of the famous Drau Cycle Path (Drauradweg), which follows the Drau river. It's well-paved, gently sloping, and incredibly scenic.

    Hiking: Take a hike up to the Pyramidenkogel observation tower for a 360-degree view of the Carinthian lakes and the Alps.

Day 8: Carinthia to Olomouc, Czechia

    Drive: ~520 km (approx. 6 hours).

    The Route: Start the journey back north, passing Vienna and heading into the historic Czech city of Olomouc.

    Activity: Olomouc is a hidden gem with a beautiful town square (often called a "mini Prague" without the crowds). It's a great place to stretch your legs with an evening city walk and enjoy some hearty Czech food before your final leg. Supercharger available right in the city.

Day 9: Olomouc to Warsaw

    Drive: ~480 km (approx. 5.5 hours).

    The Route: Easy highway driving back through Ostrava and central Poland to arrive home by the late afternoon.
