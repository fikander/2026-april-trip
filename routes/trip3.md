# The Carpathian & Danube Loop

Overview: Warsaw → Kraków (PL) → High Tatras (SK) → Slovak Paradise (SK) → Budapest (HU) → Bratislava (SK) → Warsaw

A mountain-and-city route that trades Alpine lakes for Carpathian peaks, iron-ladder gorge scrambles, and Danube cycling. Two capital cities with world-class food scenes, thermal baths, and nightlife.

## Day 1 — Fri 18 Apr
Warsaw → Kraków
~300 km · ~3.5h drive

Head south on the A1/E75. Arrive early afternoon. Kraków's Old Town, Wawel Castle, and Kazimierz district are all walkable. Grab dinner in the Jewish Quarter.
Sleep: Kraków

## Day 2 — Sat 19 Apr
Kraków — Vistula cycling + Zakopane day trip
Cycling ~30–40 km morning, drive to Zakopane ~100 km

Morning: cycle the Vistula Greenway trail heading west from Kraków — flat, paved, riverside. Afternoon: drive south to Zakopane at the foot of the Tatras. Walk Krupówki street, take the funicular up Gubałówka for sunset panorama of the Tatra ridge.
Sleep: Zakopane or Kraków

## Day 3 — Sun 20 Apr
Zakopane → High Tatras, Slovakia
~80 km · ~1.5h drive

Cross into Slovakia via Łysa Polana. Base yourself in Štrbské Pleso or Tatranská Lomnica. Afternoon hike: Štrbské Pleso lake loop (easy, 1.5h) or take the cable car to Skalnaté Pleso (1,751m) for panoramic views.
Tesla Tip: Supercharger in Poprad, just below the Tatras.
Sleep: High Tatras

## Day 4 — Mon 21 Apr
High Tatras — hiking + cycling
No driving

Hiking: The classic Tatras ridge hike from Hrebienok to Zamkovského chata, or the gentler Tatranská Magistrála trail with mountain hut stops. Cycling: afternoon ride on the Tatra cycling path connecting the resort villages — paved, mostly flat, with mountain backdrops.
Sleep: High Tatras

## Day 5 — Tue 22 Apr
High Tatras → Slovak Paradise National Park
~80 km · ~1h drive

Short drive south to Slovenský Raj (Slovak Paradise). This park is famous for its technical gorge hikes using iron ladders, chains, and wooden bridges through waterfalls. Hike the Suchá Belá gorge (2–3h, moderate) — one of the most spectacular canyon walks in Europe.
Sleep: Čingov or Podlesok (park edge)

## Day 6 — Wed 23 Apr
Slovak Paradise → Budapest
~350 km · ~4h drive

South through the Hungarian plains. Arrive in Budapest mid-afternoon. Walk the Danube promenade, cross the Chain Bridge, ride up to Fisherman's Bastion for the view. Evening: ruin bars in the Jewish Quarter or a long soak in Széchenyi thermal baths.
Tesla Tip: Superchargers in Miskolc and Budapest.
Sleep: Budapest

## Day 7 — Thu 24 Apr
Budapest — Danube cycling + city
Cycling ~40–50 km

Cycle Margaret Island and the Danube riverside paths, or take the bikes to Szentendre (charming artist village 20 km north, flat bike path along the river). Afternoon: Hungarian Parliament, Gellért thermal baths, Central Market Hall. Budapest's food scene is incredible — try a modern Hungarian tasting menu.
Sleep: Budapest

## Day 8 — Fri 25 Apr
Budapest → Bratislava
~200 km · ~2.5h drive (or cycle part!)

Drive (or partly cycle) along the Danube toward Bratislava. The EuroVelo 6 cycle path follows the river and is beautifully flat. If cycling a section, the stretch around Gabčíkovo dam is scenic. Bratislava's compact old town is perfect for a final evening walk — climb to the castle for sunset over the Danube.
Tesla Tip: Supercharger in Bratislava.
Sleep: Bratislava

## Day 9 — Sat 26 Apr
Bratislava → Warsaw
~660 km · ~6.5h drive

The longest drive day. North through Czechia (Brno → Ostrava) or via Slovakia and southern Poland. Break the drive with a stop in Olomouc or Katowice. Home by evening.
