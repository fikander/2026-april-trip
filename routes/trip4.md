# The Baltic & Lakeland Explorer

Overview: Warsaw → Masuria (PL) → Gdańsk (PL) → Słowiński NP (PL) → Toruń (PL) → Wrocław (PL) → Warsaw

The anti-Alps trip. Instead of heading south, go north to Poland's Great Masurian Lakes and Baltic coast. Flat cycling paradise, kayaking rivers, massive sand dunes, medieval Hanseatic cities, and empty beaches in spring. This is the trip for people who want to discover Poland itself.

## Day 1 — Fri 18 Apr
Warsaw → Masuria (Giżycko / Mikołajki)
~260 km · ~3.5h drive

Northeast past Ostrołęka into the Masurian Lake District. Over 2,000 lakes connected by rivers and canals. Base in Giżycko (on Lake Niegocin) or Mikołajki. Late-afternoon walk along the waterfront. Birdlife is incredible in April — ospreys, white-tailed eagles, cranes.
Sleep: Giżycko or Mikołajki

## Day 2 — Sat 19 Apr
Masuria — cycling the lake trails
Cycling ~40–60 km

Cycle the lakeside trails around the Great Masurian Lakes. The route from Giżycko through the forest to Ryn (with its Teutonic castle) and onward along Lake Śniardwy is flat, quiet, and stunning. Many sections on dedicated bike paths through pine forests. Optional: rent a kayak for an afternoon paddle on the Krutynia River.
Sleep: Giżycko or Mikołajki

## Day 3 — Sun 20 Apr
Masuria → Gdańsk
~310 km · ~3.5h drive

West and north to the Baltic coast. Gdańsk's historic center is one of the most beautiful in Europe — rebuilt after WWII, the Long Market and waterfront are jaw-dropping. Walk the Motława river embankment, visit the European Solidarity Centre. Amazing seafood and craft beer scene.
Sleep: Gdańsk

## Day 4 — Mon 21 Apr
Gdańsk — Hel Peninsula cycling
Cycling ~35–50 km

Drive or take the ferry to the Hel Peninsula — a 35 km-long sand spit jutting into the Baltic. Cycle the bike path that runs the entire length: sea on both sides, pine forest, and fishing villages. It's flat and car-free for most of the route. The town of Hel at the tip has seal sanctuaries and fish shacks.
Sleep: Gdańsk

## Day 5 — Tue 22 Apr
Gdańsk → Słowiński National Park
~150 km · ~2h drive

West along the coast to Łeba and Słowiński NP. This park has Europe's largest shifting sand dunes — the "Polish Sahara." Hike through coastal forest to the Łącka dune (42m high) for surreal desert-meets-Baltic views. In April you'll likely have it almost to yourself.
Sleep: Łeba

## Day 6 — Wed 23 Apr
Słowiński — coastal hiking + cycling
No driving

Morning: hike the park's coastal trail from Rąbka to the lighthouse and dunes (~12 km round trip through forest and beach). Afternoon: cycle inland along the Łeba river trail through marshland — flat, peaceful, prime birdwatching territory.
Sleep: Łeba

## Day 7 — Thu 24 Apr
Łeba → Toruń
~280 km · ~3.5h drive

South to the medieval city of Toruń — birthplace of Copernicus, UNESCO World Heritage, and famous for its gingerbread. The old town is entirely built of red brick and sits on the Vistula. Walk the city walls, visit a gingerbread workshop, climb the leaning tower.
Sleep: Toruń

## Day 8 — Fri 25 Apr
Toruń → Wrocław
~270 km · ~3h drive
Cycling ~20–30 km in Wrocław

South to Wrocław — the city of 100+ bridges and 12 islands. Afternoon cycling along the Odra river islands and through Szczytnicki Park to the stunning Centennial Hall (UNESCO). Count the city's famous dwarf statues scattered around the old town. Vibrant food and bar scene in the evening.
Sleep: Wrocław

## Day 9 — Sat 26 Apr
Wrocław → Warsaw
~350 km · ~3.5h drive

The easiest final drive of any trip option. A2/E30 motorway straight back to Warsaw. Arrive home by early afternoon with the whole evening free.
